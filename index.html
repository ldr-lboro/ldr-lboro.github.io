Language & Data

research group

Hi, welcome to LDR! We are a research group in Computer Science in the School of Science at Loughborough University.

We work at the intersection of Artificial Intelligence, natural language processing and sustainability, conducting research that is ethical, inclusive, internationally excellent and impactful.

Much of our work is inter- and transdisciplinary. We are keen to work with other academic institutions, businesses and public sector organisations to inspire, create and embed research.

Research

Renewable Energy and Sustainability

We are very proud to co-lead two amazing EPSRC/NERC-funded Centres for Doctoral Training: in Offshore Wind Energy and the Environment and Offshore Wind Energy Sustainability and Resilience. We are very active in the offshore wind area researching AI optimisation of Operations & Maintenance and physics-informed structural health monitoring. Funding has generously from from the Supergen ORE Hub, the Innovation Launchpad Network+ and the German government's Green Talents programme for a collaboration with BIBA at Uni Bremen on ice detection.

Resilience and Natural Hazards

A number of our projects combine remote sensing methodologies with social sensing, eg social media, to combine AI-based forecasting with traditional simulation methods, such as hydro-physical simulation for flood forecasting. We have worked with partners at Cefas to monitor water quality in the North and Irish Sea via the occurence of algal blooms. We are active in the area of flood modelling, led by Lydia Bryan-Smith. We are currently looking to expand our activities in this area. We ran a fun hackathon back in the day Uni Hull.

Low-resource languages

We found in recent years that leading LLMs are unable to represent and model the sociocultural nuances in low-resource languages, eg in the area of sentiment and emotion classification for social media analysis as well as song lyrics and we really want to do something about it. Our fabulous researchers Ifeoluwa Wuraola and Oyinkansola Onwuchekwa (KKC) are researching this actively, so watch this space.

Social media and Multimodality

We have multiple active strands of research in this area including on the connection of social media and mental health and biodiversity conversation. Most recently we focus on multimodal content, including memes, and how text and images work together to create meaning. The team led a highly competitive entry to SemEval and Victoria Sherratt won an Alan Turing Enrichment Award for her pioneering work on the large-scale unsupervised analysis of memes. We collaborate with Loughborough's Information Management group in this area.

Conversational AI, LLMs

We have been active in this area for about 15 years when the research was still called dialogue systems, focusing on natural dialogue, turn-taking and user satisfaction. Our recent work in this area has taken a critical perspective and pushed for explainable outputs and human-in-the-loop learning (with Toshiba Research in Cambridge), because we really don't want to build systems that we can't understand. You can chat with our ChatOSW prototype. Our PhD researcher Ben Chen is exploring conversational AI for sustainable behaviour change.

Decision support

We develop hybrid methodologies that bridge the performance, versatility and scalability of LLMs with the accountability and factuality of specialist expert knowledge. We have had Innovate UK funding to develop text analysis software for Spencer Engineering to optimise work flows and enhance productivity; to forecast stability prediction of new product formulations with Reckitt (driven by Joyjit Chatterjee), and create a novel smart speaker for residential care for Connexin. We also work with direct industry funding eg with Stanford University start-up Diffbot on a novel hierarchical learning method for deep text analysis with limited computation.

Our Loughborough Team

Prof Nina Dethlefs



Professor of Artificial intelligence with a broad interest in natural language processing, specifically generation, interaction, conversional systems, social media, and anything that applies AI towards challenges in sustainability, climate change, renewable energy and the environment.

Dr Venkata Maruti Viswanath Gunturi



Lecturer with an interest in geospatial data science, spatio-temporal data mining, urban transportation, sustainability and graph algorithms -- and a widening interest in broader AI, including LLMs. Viswanath is still at the University of Hull.

Dr Joyjit Chatterjee


Visiting academic (industry specialist), currently at EPAM Systems, invited to drive our research embedding and impact creation activities. Joyjit is a Forbes 30 Under 30 Europe lister (Mfg. and Industry), and develops AI products that bolster energy and manufacturing processes. Joyjit holds a PhD in the area of offshore wind and is a Green Talents awardee.

Ben Chen



First year PhD student researching the use of large language models and conversational AI to affect positive behaviour change through interaction. Ben holds an MSc in Human Computer Interaction from the University of Birmingham and explores the extent to which LLMs can replicate social interaction.

Callum Rothon



Third year PhD student with the Aura CDT in Offshore Wind Energy and the Environment, developing novel techniques in computer vision and physics-based machine learning for structural health monitoring of wind turbine blades. Callum previously completed a funded internship with Toshiba Research in Cambridge.

Victoria Sherratt



Third year PhD student and Turing Community Enrichment Awardee researching the semiotics of internet memes and specifically how memes draw on a combination of modalities and social and cultural context to create meanings far beyond the literal.


We also continue to collaborate actively with our colleagues at the University of Hull who finish off projects in our wonderful
Big Data Analytics Research Group (BDA).

Sedat Dogan



Third year PhD student investigating the interaction of modalities in online memes. Sedat develops novel methods to scale virality analysis beyond labelled benchmarks, while achieving feature transparency and explainablity. He was one of the drivers of our BDA SemEval 2024 entry.

Erv Mumdzjans



Third year PhD student researching the ethical generation of AI-based music. Erv's goal is to draw on music theory to create novel melodies across musical genres without infringing artists' copyright.

Oyinkansola (KKC) Onwuchekwa



Second year PhD student, and award-winning musician, KKC works to identify emotions in song lyrics, particularly AfroBeats, which mixes English with Nigerian Pidgin, Yoruba, Igbo and Hausa. She researches novel methods that can overcome the challenges of multilingual LLMs and represent nuanced meanings.

Sam Rose



Third year PhD student currently on a Turing Institute internship with EverybodyCounts. Sam works on identifying dyslexia from text, an adventurous undertaking given the irregularities in English spelling. He also redefined the notion of grapheme in natural language processing along the way.

Eamonn Tuton



Third year PhD student on the Aura CDT. Eamonn works towards digital twins for Operations & Maintenance, specifically researching how physics can make up for data sparsity around specific operational conditions, such as extreme weather.

Ifeoluwa Wuraola



Third year PhD student researching climate change discourse on social media from a comparative perspective on Nigeria and the UK. Ifeoluwa is improving methods for slang detection and understanding in low-resource languages towards better inter-cultural communication and NLP resources.
